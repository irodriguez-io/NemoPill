# Non-Functional Requirements

## How To Use This File

- Capture the qualities the system must exhibit independent of any specific feature.
- Prefer numeric, observable targets over adjectives. If the agent cannot verify it, rewrite it.
- Keep targets aligned with the goals in `01_product_vision_and_scope.md` and the architecture in `04_solution_architecture.md`.
- If a category does not apply, write `Not applicable` and explain why in one sentence.

## Availability And Reliability

The framework template's `Service level objective`, `Acceptable error budget`, `Recovery time objective (RTO)`, and `Recovery point objective (RPO)` rows are server-side vocabulary that does not map onto NemoPill's single-device, on-device-only shape per `_context/04 § Deployment shape`. They resolve to `Not applicable` below. The substantive NemoPill reliability surface — exactly-once Reminder firing, transactional atomicity for aggregate-spanning operations, boot survival, Doze tolerance, and graceful behavior when OS permissions are revoked — lives in `Degraded-mode behavior` below and is canonically owned by `_context/02 § BR-004`, `_context/02 § BR-006`, `_context/02 § BR-007`, `_context/02 § BR-012`, and `_context/06 § F-001` / `§ F-008` / `§ F-011` / `§ F-012` / `§ F-013`.

- Service level objective: `Not applicable` — NemoPill has no remote service, no server-side flow, no monthly-uptime% surface per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (b) (no `android.permission.INTERNET`) and `_context/04 § Deployment shape`. The product-level reliability invariant is `_context/02 § BR-004` (exactly-once Reminder firing for `pending` Doses at `Dose.scheduledAt`), which is an exact-execution requirement, not an availability percentage.
- Acceptable error budget: `Not applicable` — there is no SLO to consume an error budget against per the row above. Reminder firings that miss the ±60-second tolerance from `## Performance Targets` are counted by the `_context/07 § M-004` manual Doze test on real hardware and by the M-004 Robolectric reliability tests; deviations are treated as fix-forward bugs per `_context/12 § Rollback mechanism`, not as budget burn.
- Recovery time objective (RTO): `Not applicable` — there is no off-device service to recover. The closest on-device analog is Room database integrity: `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Database migration strategy` pins forward-only migrations with no `fallbackToDestructiveMigration`, so a failed migration crashes the app and is treated as a P0 fix-forward bug per `_context/12 § Rollback mechanism`; recovery time then becomes the Play Console review-and-rollout time, which is upstream of NemoPill and not a target this file can pin.
- Recovery point objective (RPO): `Not applicable` — NemoPill does not back up Patient data off-device. `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (a) pins `android:allowBackup="false"` and `android:dataExtractionRules="@xml/data_extraction_rules"` excluding all app data from `<cloud-backup>` and `<device-transfer>`, intentionally operationalizing the `_context/05 § Data protection` no-off-device-transmission rule. The Patient's data lives in the device's app-private Room database; if the device is lost or factory-reset, the data is gone by design — `_context/06 § Data Ownership And Retention` documents "uninstall = total deletion" as a feature, not a recoverability gap.
- Degraded-mode behavior:
    - **OS-level Doze / App Standby / battery optimization.** Reminders continue to fire via `AlarmManager.setAlarmClock` per `_context/04 § Technology And Runtime Decisions § Exact-time scheduling` and the dual-strategy `AlarmManager` + `WorkManager` design per `_context/04 § Technology And Runtime Decisions § Deferred / periodic background work`. The 1-hour BR-010 late-Reminder window per `_context/06 § BR-010` is the defense-in-depth backstop for any Doze-induced delay; outside that window the `pending → missed` transition runs on its normal schedule. Validated by the M-002 walking-skeleton hardware test and the M-004 manual Doze test per `_context/07 § M-002` / `§ M-004`.
    - **Device reboot.** Manifest-declared `BootCompletedReceiver` per `_context/04 § Module Or Component Map` re-registers all `pending` Doses whose `scheduledAt` is in the future or within the BR-010 1-hour late window per `_context/04 § Interface And Adapter Strategy § SchedulerPort` and `_context/06 § F-011` boot-survival re-registration. Validated by M-002 done-when item (6) and M-004 done-when item (7) per `_context/07 § Milestone Register`.
    - **App update.** Per `_context/03 § EC-004`, on the post-update first-launch path the Scheduler re-registers the alarm so the pending Reminder still fires — verified by an integration test per `_context/05 § Test Portfolio § Integration tests`.
    - **`POST_NOTIFICATIONS` permission revoked at runtime (Android 13+).** Per `_context/03 § EC-001` and `_context/06 § F-005` failure handling, in-app Dose list continues to surface `pending` Doses with action buttons; a non-blocking banner offers to re-request the permission on next foreground. The `Result.Err.NotificationsPermissionRevoked` UX affordance per `_context/07 § M-004` done-when item (7) renders the message; the Reminder presentation path is degraded but the Confirmation path is not.
    - **`SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM` permission revoked at runtime (Android 12+ / 13+).** Per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (b), the API 33+ `USE_EXACT_ALARM` declaration is non-revocable so the degraded path applies only to the API 31–32 `SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM` window. The `Result.Err.ExactAlarmPermissionRevoked` UX affordance per `_context/07 § M-004` done-when item (7) surfaces the failure; the in-app Dose list and retroactive Confirmation continue to work; only the exact-time alarm registration is lost. Inexact `WorkManager` periodic work continues per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (a).
    - **Manual device-clock change without timezone change.** Per `_context/03 § EC-003`, the Scheduler detects the clock jump via the boot-complete / Doze-exit / app-foreground reconciliation paths defined in BR-004 and BR-010; no duplicate Reminders, no silent failures. Validated by an integration test per `_context/05 § Test Portfolio § Integration tests`.
    - **Two Medications scheduled at the same wall-clock time.** Per `_context/03 § EC-002`, two distinct Reminders fire with independent action buttons. Adherence counts each Dose independently per `_context/02 § BR-008`.
    - **DST fall-back duplicate hour.** Per `_context/03 § EC-005` and `_context/02 § BR-012`, the Reminder fires exactly once at the first occurrence.
    - **`BroadcastReceiver.goAsync()` timeout.** Per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (c), every async receiver block is wrapped in `withTimeout(8_000)`; if the block exceeds 8 seconds, the framework cancellation runs and the OS-level 10-second hard kill does not fire. The failure surfaces as a `Result.Err.Unexpected` per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`'s minimal logging contract.
    - **Concurrent Confirmation taps on the same Dose.** Per `_context/03 § EC-006`, the Dose ↔ Confirmation 1:1 invariant holds across the lock-screen action and the in-app card path; the `room.withTransaction { }` boundary per `_context/04 § Trade-off accepted: the Dose aggregate's transaction spans two Gradle modules` is the enforcement point.
    - **Room migration failure.** Per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Database migration strategy`, the app crashes rather than silently dropping data via `fallbackToDestructiveMigration`. The Patient sees the defensive Room-migration failure screen per `_context/11 § Defensive Room-migration failure screen copy`; recovery is the next fix-forward release.

## Accessibility

- Conformance target: **WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor for every screen and notification surface; WCAG 2.1 AAA is the achieved level for `color.text.primary` and `color.text.secondary` per `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules`.** This row is locked at WCAG 2.1 AA per `_context/03 § UX Constraints And Rules § Accessibility expectations` and reaffirmed in `_context/11`; no WCAG 2.2 upgrade is committed at T-004 because no prior `_context/` file commits to 2.2 and an upgrade would require a fresh ADR superseding `_context/03` and `_context/11`. Section 508 is `Not applicable` — NemoPill is not procured by a US federal agency and falls outside Section 508's jurisdictional trigger per `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` (NIST 800-53 row, by adjacency).
- Required input modalities:
    - **Touch** — primary modality for the Patient persona per `_context/03 § Personas Or Actors § Patient` ("one-handed friendly"); every Compose surface is touch-first.
    - **TalkBack (screen reader)** — required for `_context/03 § Personas Or Actors § Patient` ("screen-reader compatible (TalkBack)") and the M-006 close-out walkthrough per `_context/07 § M-006 Done When` item (6). Every actionable element exposes a programmatic role and an accessible label using the Ubiquitous Language from `_context/01 § Ubiquitous Language` ("Confirm Dose taken" rather than just "Taken"); status changes (Dose flipping `pending → taken`, Reminder firing) are announced via Compose accessibility live regions per `_context/03 § Accessibility expectations`.
    - **Hardware keyboard** — supported via Material 3 / Compose defaults (focus traversal, focus ring per `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules` row 5). Not a primary modality; not a separate verification surface in MVP.
    - **Voice (Voice Access / Voice Control)** — supported via Material 3 / Compose defaults; not a separate verification surface in MVP.
- Required visual contrast and sizing rules:
    - **Normal text (< 18 pt regular / < 14 pt bold)** on `color.surface` or `color.surface.muted`: ≥ 4.5:1 (WCAG 1.4.3 AA). `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules` row 1 documents that `color.text.primary` achieves 16.2:1 (light) / 16.6:1 (dark) — both AAA — and `color.text.secondary` achieves 7.5:1 (light) / 7.0:1 (dark) — both AAA.
    - **Large text (≥ 18 pt regular / ≥ 14 pt bold)** on `color.surface` or `color.surface.muted`: ≥ 3.0:1 (WCAG 1.4.3 AA). Every `_context/11` color-system token passes.
    - **Non-text UI components and graphical objects** (icons, focus rings, input borders, checkboxes, divider lines): ≥ 3.0:1 against adjacent surface (WCAG 1.4.11 AA). `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules` row 3 documents the `color.border` compensating control for the cases where the raw border value would otherwise fail.
    - **Focus ring** (WCAG 2.4.11 enhanced): `color.accent` ≥ 3.0:1 against both the focused element and the adjacent surface per `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules` row 5.
    - **Disabled control text** (NemoPill self-imposed floor): ≥ 3.0:1 via `color.text.secondary` at 50% opacity per `_context/11 § Color System § Contrast rules` row 4.
    - **Tap target minimum**: 48 dp × 48 dp on every interactive Compose element, enforced via `Modifier.minimumInteractiveComponentSize()` per `_context/11 § Components` (Checkbox, Switch, RadioButton, Chip rows). `_context/03 § Accessibility expectations` and `_context/11 § Iconography And Imagery` reaffirm the 48 dp floor.
    - **System font-size scaling**: honor the device font-size setting up to and including Android "Largest" without text truncation in the day's Dose list, the Reminder notification body, or the Adherence summary per `_context/03 § Accessibility expectations`.
    - **Color-blindness defense-in-depth**: information is never conveyed by color alone (WCAG 1.4.1). Hyperlinks within `text.body` use `color.accent` plus a 1 dp underline per `_context/11 § Typography § Underline rule`; Dose status is conveyed by both color and text label / icon, never color alone.
    - **Vibration alongside sound** for Reminders per `_context/03 § Accessibility expectations`, for hearing-impaired Patients and Patients in low-volume environments.
- Internationalization scope:
    - **UI copy**: English-only in MVP per `_context/03 § UX Constraints And Rules § Content or copy rules` ("Localization: English-only for MVP — no other locales planned in this phase"). No alternate UI locale, no RTL support, no per-locale string fork.
    - **Privacy Policy**: bilingual English + Spanish per `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` (CCPA / CPRA and Latin American national data-protection laws rows). English substance is authored in `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance` and lives at `legal/PRIVACY_POLICY.en.md` per `_context/05 § Evidence And Audit Trail`. Spanish translation is deferred to M-006 per `_context/11 § Spanish Privacy Policy translation` and lives at `legal/PRIVACY_POLICY.es.md` per `_context/05 § Evidence And Audit Trail`.
    - **Date / time / wall-clock formatting**: device-locale-driven via standard Android `android.text.format.DateFormat` and `java.time.format.DateTimeFormatter` APIs per `_context/04 § Technology And Runtime Decisions § Implementation language` (Kotlin/JVM `java.time`). `_context/02 § BR-012`'s timezone-handling rules are independent of locale formatting — they apply regardless of which locale renders the wall-clock string.

## Observability And Operability

The framework template's metrics / tracing / alerting / health-check vocabulary is server-side scaffolding that does not map onto NemoPill's on-device-only shape per `_context/04 § Deployment shape` and `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`. The two rows that carry substantive content — `Logging baseline` and `Required metrics` — are quantified against the on-device-only reality, and a deliberately constrained crash-reporting path is the only non-`Not applicable` operability surface. The substantive observability content for NemoPill is the **two-tier error policy** from `_context/04 § Functional Programming Rules § Error handling rule`: `Result.Err` for expected business-rule failures (never logged, surfaced as Patient-facing UX copy) and `Result.Err.Unexpected` for genuine surprises (logged minimally on-device, reported via Play Console Android Vitals per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`). ADR-067 records the on-device-only-logging contract.

- Logging baseline:
    - **What gets logged.** Only the `Result.Err.Unexpected` branch of the two-tier error policy per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations` — via `Log.e` for genuine errors and `Log.w` for recoverable adapter-level surprises that nonetheless propagate up as `Unexpected`. Expected business-rule failures (`BR-002` invalid date range, `BR-003` zero/negative quantity, `BR-011` retroactive-window expired, `BR-001` atomic-replace conflict, `BR-007` archive-with-future-pending atomicity failure) are explicitly **never logged** per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations` — they are returned as `Result.Err` to the Application boundary and surfaced as non-technical UX copy per `_context/03 § Content or copy rules`.
    - **Tag convention.** `NemoPill.<module>.<class>` per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations` (e.g., `NemoPill.scheduling.AlarmManagerSchedulerAdapter`). `logcat` filtering by `NemoPill.*` retrieves only this app's surfaces.
    - **No Patient data in log lines.** Per `_context/06 § Error Handling And Recovery`'s "no Patient data in failure evidence" rule, every log line is either a Patient-data-free constant or contains only values derived from build-time constants and non-Patient state. Enforced by `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`'s Konsist rules (i) no `throw` statement uses Kotlin string templates or `String.format` / `StringBuilder.append` in its message argument and (ii) every Domain `data class` (`Medication`, `Dose`, `Confirmation`, plus `Strength` / `Form` / `TimeOfDay` / `DateRange` / `Frequency` / `DoseQuantity`) overrides `toString()` to a redacted form.
    - **Structured fields.** `Not applicable` for log structure beyond Android's standard `Log.e(tag, message, throwable?)` shape. There is no log aggregator, no centralized index, no field-level query surface, so structured JSON would add cost without reader value. The on-device `logcat` is the consumption surface during development; in production the only consumed surface is the redacted stack trace inside the Play Console Android Vitals report.
    - **Retention.** On-device: bounded by Android's `logcat` ring buffer (≈ 1 MB per buffer, OS-managed, no project control). Play Console Android Vitals: retained by Google per their internal policy (≈ 60 days for raw crash detail at MVP authoring, longer for aggregated counters). Not a project-controlled retention surface per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`.
    - **Sampling.** `Not applicable` — log volume is too low to require sampling. The volume cap is set by `Result.Err.Unexpected` occurrence rate, which is per definition rare.
    - **No third-party logging SDK.** Per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations` ("No analytics SDK in MVP"), there is no Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Datadog Logs, Sentry breadcrumbs, or equivalent on the classpath. The Konsist `arch-conformance` rule from `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § arch-conformance` that forbids `java.net.*` / `okhttp3.*` / `retrofit2.*` imports is the defense-in-depth backstop — any logging SDK that needs to phone home would fail the rule.
- Required metrics:
    - **Service-side metrics: `Not applicable`** — NemoPill has no service surface per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`. The framework template's golden-signals vocabulary (latency, traffic, errors, saturation) is server-side scaffolding with no clientside analog.
    - **Business KPIs are owned elsewhere.** The file-01 Reminder-to-Confirmation rate and the `_context/02 § BR-008` Adherence percentage are **product features** surfaced to the Patient via `_context/03 § UC-009`, computed on demand by `_context/04 § AdherenceCalculator` — they are not observability metrics piped to a backend. The file-12 § Monitoring And On-Call decision keeps them where they live (the Patient's screen) and explicitly does **not** lift them into a metrics backend.
    - **Crash and ANR signals: Google Play Console Android Vitals** per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`. Google Play Services collects stack-trace-plus-device-metadata payloads on Patient devices (with Patient-opt-out at the OS level via Google Play Services → Usage & diagnostics) and reports them to the project's Play Console. No `android.permission.INTERNET` required in the NemoPill manifest per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call` — Google Play Services has its own network permission set. Payloads contain no app-private state because of the Konsist redaction rules above.
    - **No custom in-app metrics emission.** No StatsD, OpenTelemetry, Prometheus pushgateway, Firebase Performance Monitoring, or equivalent. The Konsist no-network-imports rule from `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § arch-conformance` is the enforcement.
- Tracing baseline: `Not applicable` — NemoPill is a single-process, single-device, no-network application per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`. The framework template's "request IDs / job IDs / correlation IDs" vocabulary is server-side scaffolding with no clientside analog. Intra-app, every `Dose` / `Confirmation` / domain event already carries the relevant aggregate IDs (`MedicationId`, `DoseId`, `DoseScheduleId`, `ConfirmationId` per `_context/04 § Functional Programming Rules § Shared utility rule`); a developer chasing a bug uses those IDs directly, not a synthetic correlation-ID layer.
- Dashboards required at launch:

## Security NFRs

The substantive security surface is fully owned by `_context/05 § Security Guardrails` (six rule areas: Authentication / Input validation / Secret management / Data protection / Dependency and supply chain / OS permissions and IPC boundaries) and operationalized by `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` (no off-device backup; upload-key custody) and `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § secret-scan` / `§ arch-conformance` (Konsist + gitleaks enforcement). The rows below restate the file-05 / file-12 decisions in the framework template's NFR vocabulary — they do not introduce new security policy.

- Authentication strength:
    - **Primary boundary**: the **Android OS lock screen** per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization` ("anyone with physical possession of an unlocked device is treated as the Patient"). No app-level authentication is required by default; the device lock screen is the contract.
    - **Optional Patient-configurable app-level gate**: `androidx.biometric.BiometricPrompt` (biometric or device-credential fallback) per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization`. Default: **off**. When enabled, the gate must succeed before the app surfaces any Medication / Dose / Confirmation data; the gate re-engages after a Patient-configurable inactivity timeout. Configured in Settings per `_context/11 § Biometric-gate Settings group copy`.
    - **No multi-factor, no SSO, no SAML, no OIDC, no API keys, no mTLS** — `Not applicable` per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization` (no multi-user, no caregiver, no role-based authorization; the single Patient operates against their own device).
    - **No per-screen or per-action re-authentication** — only the inactivity-timeout-driven re-engagement of the optional gate per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization`.
- Session and token lifetimes:
    - **`Not applicable` for sessions / tokens / refresh policy** — NemoPill has no auth token, no OAuth flow, no refresh token, no API-key rotation per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization` (there is no service to authenticate against). The framework template's "max idle / absolute / refresh policy" vocabulary is server-side scaffolding with no clientside analog.
    - **Patient-configurable biometric-gate inactivity timeout** is the only timeout NemoPill exposes per `_context/05 § Authentication and authorization`. The default value and the configurable range are locked in `_context/11 § Biometric-gate Settings group copy`. Triggering the timeout re-engages the gate; it does not invalidate any token (because there is none).
- Encryption in transit:
    - **`Not applicable`** — NemoPill performs no network I/O per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (b) (no `android.permission.INTERNET`, no HTTP / WebSocket / gRPC / DNS client code). Enforced by the `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § arch-conformance` Konsist rule that forbids `java.net.*` / `okhttp3.*` / `retrofit2.*` imports anywhere in `_source/` and asserts `android.permission.INTERNET` is absent from the merged manifest.
    - **Forward-looking**: if a future product decision introduces a network surface (e.g., post-MVP caregiver-share feature, post-MVP server-side adherence aggregation), the in-transit floor is **TLS 1.3 minimum** with no fallback to TLS 1.2 or earlier; the decision lands as a fresh ADR superseding the current `Not applicable` resolution.
- Encryption at rest:
    - **Room database**: **not encrypted in MVP** (no SQLCipher dependency on the classpath) per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (a). The Android app-sandbox plus the device OS lock screen are the protection boundary; under the device-locked threat model that underpins the file-05 authentication row, encrypted-at-rest yields marginal incremental protection at the cost of a real dependency, a non-trivial cold-start, and a passphrase-management problem.
    - **Android Auto Backup and device-to-device transfer**: **disabled** per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (a) — `android:allowBackup="false"` (API ≤ 30) plus `android:dataExtractionRules` excluding all app data from both `<cloud-backup>` and `<device-transfer>` (API 31+). The encryption-at-rest surface is therefore the Patient's own device, not Google's cloud-backup infrastructure.
    - **App-private internal storage**: the Room database file lives at `/data/data/<package>/databases/` on the device, sandboxed by Android per `_context/12 § Infrastructure § Datastores in use` and `_context/06 § Data Ownership And Retention`. Removed by Android on uninstall per `_context/06 § Data Ownership And Retention` ("uninstall = total deletion").
    - **Forward-looking rule for future runtime secrets**: per `_context/05 § Secret management`, any local-only runtime secret introduced post-MVP must be persisted via `androidx.security.crypto.EncryptedSharedPreferences` or directly in the Android Keystore — never plain `SharedPreferences`, never a plain Room column, never an in-memory `companion object` constant. Enforced by a Konsist rule asserted in `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § arch-conformance`.
- Vulnerability response SLA (fix-forward only, no rollback per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Rollback mechanism`):
    - **P0 (critical) — exploitable security vulnerability, Patient-data exposure path, or any class of failure that causes Reminders to silently stop firing for any Patient who installed the affected release**: **same-day fix-forward if the Human gatekeeper is available**; otherwise the next operating session per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Rollback mechanism`. Concrete steps: bump `versionCode`, fix on a branch, run the full 8-stage CI pipeline per `_context/12 § Continuous Integration` (no skipped checks under time pressure), promote through Internal Testing → Production via the M-006 cut-release runbook per `_context/12 § Operational Runbooks`.
    - **P1 (high) — vulnerability with material Patient-data risk but no immediate exploitation path, or material reliability regression detected via Android Vitals**: **within the work-week** per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Rollback mechanism`. Same fix-forward sequence as P0.
    - **P2 (medium / low) — non-exploitable defect, dependency advisory with no in-codebase impact, cosmetic security hardening**: **next normal release** per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Rollback mechanism`. Bundled with normal feature delivery.
    - **No staged rollout, no halt-rollout safety net** per `_context/12 § Continuous Delivery § Deployment topology` — Production releases ship to 100% of eligible Patients immediately. The trade-off is documented in `_context/12 § DevOps Risks And Trade-Offs`; fix-forward is the single recovery path.
    - **Manual dependency review baseline** per `_context/05 § Dependency and supply chain` — every new Gradle dependency (or transitive update worth reviewing) commits with a one-line justification in the commit message. **Automated CVE scanning (OWASP dependency-check or equivalent) is deferred** per `_context/05 § Dependency and supply chain`; if a vulnerability is reported against a pinned dependency before the deferral closes, response follows the P0 / P1 / P2 framing above based on severity and exploitability against NemoPill's surface (which is small — no `INTERNET`, no IPC export beyond the documented `BootCompletedReceiver`, no payment surface, no auth surface beyond the optional biometric gate).
    - **Secret-scanner (gitleaks) and `arch-conformance` (Konsist) are the in-pipeline backstop** per `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § secret-scan` and `§ arch-conformance` — any commit that lands a credential-shaped string, an `INTERNET` permission, or a network-API import fails the merge gate.

## Privacy NFRs

Privacy is one of NemoPill's load-bearing product properties, not an afterthought — the entire architecture (no `android.permission.INTERNET` per `_context/05 § Data protection`; no Android Auto Backup per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets`; no analytics SDK per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`; no export and no import per `_context/05 § Data protection` and file-09 ADRs) is calibrated to honor the `_context/01 § Business Constraints And Assumptions` commitment that medication data must not be transmitted or shared without explicit Patient consent. The rows below restate the existing privacy posture in NFR vocabulary.

- Data minimization rule:
    - **Collect nothing beyond what the Patient enters into the in-app forms.** Every Patient datum (`Medication` name / Strength / Form, `Dose Schedule` Frequency / TimesOfDay / DateRange, `Confirmation` taken/skipped status and `confirmedAt` timestamp) originates from a Patient action and is stored only in the device-local Room database per `_context/04 § Local persistence` and `_context/12 § Infrastructure § Datastores in use`. No background collection, no derivative collection, no Patient profiling.
    - **No analytics SDK, no third-party telemetry, no remote logging** per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations` ("No analytics SDK in MVP") and `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`. Google Play Console Android Vitals is the sole remote evidence stream; payloads are anonymized stack-trace-plus-device-metadata per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`, contain no Patient data per the Konsist redaction rules from `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`, and are subject to Patient OS-level opt-out via Google Play Services → Usage & diagnostics per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`.
    - **No tracking SDK, no advertising ID use, no fingerprinting**. NemoPill does not declare `com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID`, does not request `READ_PHONE_STATE`, does not query `Settings.Secure.ANDROID_ID` or `Build.SERIAL`, does not link Patient activity to any device identifier per `_context/05 § OS permissions and IPC boundaries` (the minimum-permission manifest forbids permissions outside the explicit allow-list).
    - **No payment data, no card data, no PII beyond medication context**. `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` PCI DSS row confirms there is no cardholder data scope. No Patient name field, no contact information, no insurance data, no clinician identifier.
- User-facing data rights (operationalized for the Patient via in-app affordances; the bilingual Privacy Policy authored per `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` and `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance` is the textual statement of these rights):
    - **Right to access (right to know)** — every Medication, Dose Schedule, Dose, and Confirmation the Patient has ever entered is readable inside the app via `_context/03 § UC-008` (today's Dose list) and `_context/03 § UC-009` (Adherence history) per `_context/05 § CCPA / CPRA` row. There is no separate "data download" surface because there is no separate copy of the data to download — the in-app UI **is** the canonical view.
    - **Right to correction** — every Medication and Dose Schedule has an in-app edit affordance per `_context/03 § UC-002` (Replace Dose Schedule) per `_context/05 § CCPA / CPRA` row; every Confirmation has the retroactive 24-hour correction window per `_context/02 § BR-011` and `_context/03 § UC-006` / `§ UC-007`.
    - **Right to deletion (Patient-controlled hard delete)** — Settings → "Delete all my data" per `_context/06 § F-014` and `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (c). **Irreversible**: there is no recovery, no backup, no undo per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (c). Guarded by a deliberate-confirmation interaction (typed phrase or two-step tap) per `_context/11 § Hard-delete confirmation dialog copy`. After confirmation, the action drops every Room table per `_context/06 § F-014`, invokes `SchedulerPort.cancelAll()` per `_context/07 § M-006 Done When` item (2), and resets app state. Verified by the M-006 integration test asserting post-delete database is empty and zero `AlarmManager` registrations remain per `_context/07 § M-006 Done When` item (2).
    - **Right to portability (data export)**: **`Not applicable` in MVP** — `_context/05 § Data protection` explicitly excludes any export feature on the rationale that an export file is itself a data-protection risk (it could be accidentally shared); a fresh ADR superseding that decision is required to add export per the same `_context/05 § Data protection` block. The symmetric **no-import** rule applies per the file-09 ADR enumerated in `_context/07 § Dependency And Blocker Register`. Patients exercising portability under jurisdictions that require it can satisfy the right by reading their data in-app and transcribing manually; this is acknowledged as a limited substitute and recorded in the bilingual Privacy Policy per `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance`.
    - **Right to opt out of sale / sharing**: **vacuously satisfied** — NemoPill does not sell, share, or otherwise transfer Patient data to any third party per `_context/05 § CCPA / CPRA` row and `_context/05 § Google Play Data safety section` row (the Play Console declaration is "no data collected, no data shared, no data sold"). The right is restated in the bilingual Privacy Policy per `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance` for transparency.
    - **Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights**: vacuously satisfied — there are no Patient-tier distinctions, no paid features, no withheld functionality per `_context/01 § Business Constraints And Assumptions` ("Zero budget. No paid services... No commercial distribution or monetization in this phase").
    - **Consent**: the Patient's consent to process their data is implicit in their installing and entering data into the app; the bilingual Privacy Policy per `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance` discloses what processing occurs (on-device only, no transmission, no sharing). No analytics-style "accept cookies" or "share crash data" pop-up — the OS-level Google Play Services usage-and-diagnostics toggle is the only opt-out surface per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`.
    - **Data-controller contact**: disclosed in the Privacy Policy per `_context/05 § Latin American national data-protection laws` row; the contact path is GitHub Issues at `https://github.com/izirodriguez/NemoPill` per `_context/09 § ADR-061` (English Privacy Policy substance authored in file 11, contact via GitHub Issues).
- Cross-border transfer rules:
    - **`Not applicable` in the strict cross-border-transfer sense** — NemoPill performs no data transfer across borders or otherwise per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (b) (no off-device transmission). The Patient's data lives only on the Patient's own device throughout its lifecycle.
    - **Distribution scope cross-border consideration** — the app itself is distributed across Latin America (excluding Brazil) plus the United States per `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` (GDPR / UK-DPA row), so a Patient in any of those jurisdictions installs the app under their local data-protection regime. The substantive compliance work for those regimes is in `_context/05 § Compliance Applicability Matrix` (CCPA / CPRA row and Latin American national data-protection laws row); the bilingual EN + ES Privacy Policy per `_context/11 § English Privacy Policy substance` is the surface that addresses the regime-specific transparency / data-subject-rights requirements.
    - **No data residency commitment** — the Patient's data resides where the Patient's device resides; the project has no data center, no cloud region, no "data residency" lever to pull per `_context/04 § Deployment shape`. If a future cross-border-transfer compliance requirement (e.g., a Latin American regulator mandates in-country residency for health data) lands, the in-MVP architecture already satisfies it because the data never leaves the Patient's device.
    - **Google Play Services exception**: Android Vitals crash reports are collected and transmitted by Google Play Services per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`. Google Play Services is a Google-controlled cross-border surface that NemoPill does not control. The bilingual Privacy Policy discloses this per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call` ("This is mentioned in the bilingual Privacy Policy ... as part of the 'data we share with Google' disclosure") and Patients can opt out at the OS level. The exception is recorded here so future readers see the one Google-mediated cross-border path that exists.

        - Crash reporters that phone home — Firebase Crashlytics, Sentry, Bugsnag, Rollbar per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`. Android Vitals is the sanctioned crash path.
        - Analytics SDKs — Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`.
        - Push notification SDKs — Firebase Cloud Messaging — `Not applicable` to MVP per `_context/05 § Data protection` (no remote service to push from).
        - Image / asset loading libraries that fetch from URLs — Coil with `coil-network`, Glide with OkHttp integration, Picasso. Local-resource-only image loading is allowed.

## Cost And Resource Constraints

The framework template's infrastructure-budget / cost-per-transaction vocabulary is server-side scaffolding that does not map onto NemoPill's on-device-only shape per `_context/01 § Business Constraints And Assumptions` ("Zero budget. No paid services, vendors, or third-party SaaS dependencies"). The substantive resource constraints live entirely on the Patient's device and are governed by Android's runtime limits; the project-controlled cost surface collapses to a single one-time Google Play Console developer fee.

- Monthly infrastructure budget envelope:
    - **`Not applicable as a recurring infrastructure cost`** — NemoPill has no remote service, no cloud hosting, no SaaS dependency, no CDN, no managed database, no third-party crash reporter (per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`), no analytics vendor (per `_context/05 § Observability Expectations`), no message broker, no observability vendor. Recurring monthly infrastructure spend is **$0**.
    - **One-time project-controlled cost**: Google Play Console developer registration fee (**$25 USD, one-time**, paid by the Human gatekeeper Isidro at Play Console account creation, ahead of M-006 submission per `_context/07 § M-006 Done When` item (8)). This is the entire externally-paid project surface for MVP and the foreseeable post-MVP.
    - **Developer-side cost (out of scope of this register)**: the Human gatekeeper's personal Android development hardware (laptop, Android phone for the M-002 / M-004 manual Doze tests per `_context/07 § M-002` / `§ M-004`, internet connection, electricity) is paid for by Isidro and is not a project-budgeted cost. The `_context/07 § Dependency And Blocker Register` "Real Android hardware" row is the canonical statement of this hardware dependency.
    - **GitHub repository hosting**: free under GitHub's terms for the repository structure NemoPill uses per `_context/12 § Infrastructure` (single public or private repository, no GitHub Enterprise features). GitHub Actions CI runs against the free Actions minutes allowance for the relevant repo visibility per `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § CI tool` (free unlimited for public repos, 2,000 free minutes/month for private repos). The CI pipeline target of ≤ 10 minutes per `_context/12 § Continuous Integration § Average CI duration target` keeps NemoPill well inside the private-repo free tier with substantial headroom.
- Cost-per-transaction or cost-per-user expectation:
    - **`Not applicable`** — there is no transaction (no payment, no API call billed by volume, no server-side compute billed by execution) and no per-user marginal cost (the project does not pay per Patient, per device, per Reminder fired, per Confirmation recorded, or per Adherence calculation per `_context/01 § Business Constraints And Assumptions`). Adding a Patient costs the project $0 incremental.
    - **Forward-looking**: if a future product decision introduces a backend service or a third-party SaaS dependency (e.g., a caregiver-share feature, a server-side Adherence aggregation, a managed crash reporter), this row revisits to capture the per-Patient cost. Such an addition itself requires a fresh ADR superseding `_context/01 § Business Constraints And Assumptions` (Commercial constraints row) and `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (b) (no off-device transmission), so the cost-per-user surface would be introduced under explicit gatekeeper authorization rather than slip in.
- Resource limits per service:
    - **`Not applicable as a server-side surface`** — NemoPill is the only "service" and it runs in the Patient's own Android process per `_context/04 § Deployment shape`; resource limits are imposed by Android, not by the project. The framework template's CPU / memory / storage caps vocabulary is server-side scaffolding.
    - **On-device CPU**: bounded by Android process scheduling and Doze / App Standby per `_context/04 § Architecture Summary § Key architectural constraint`. The `BroadcastReceiver.goAsync()` 8-second internal cap from `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (c) (2-sec safety margin under Android's 10-sec hard kill) is the CPU-time ceiling for any background receiver block. The `WorkManager` worker p95 ≤ 8 seconds target from `## Performance Targets` is the analogous ceiling for periodic workers.
    - **On-device memory**: bounded by Android's per-process heap limit on `minSdk = 26`-class hardware per `_context/12 § Infrastructure § Android SDK targets` — the project does not pin a specific heap target because Android already enforces a per-device cap and Compose / Room's working set on `minSdk = 26` low-memory-class devices is well below the platform limit for the data volumes from Section 3 of this file (≈ 1–2 MB per active Patient-year). If a future quarter shows OOM crashes in Android Vitals per `_context/12 § Monitoring And On-Call`, this row revisits.
    - **On-device storage**: bounded by the data-volume envelope from `## Scalability And Capacity` (≈ 1–2 MB per active Patient-year, lifetime cap ≈ 10 MB even for power-user Patients across the 5-year regulator-prudent retention tail per `_context/05 § Evidence And Audit Trail`). The Room database file lives in app-private internal storage per `_context/12 § Infrastructure § Datastores in use`; no external storage permission per `_context/05 § OS permissions and IPC boundaries`.
    - **On-device battery**: a Reminder + Confirmation app should not appreciably impact battery life. The `AlarmManager.setAlarmClock` registrations per `_context/04 § Technology And Runtime Decisions § Exact-time scheduling` are inherently sparse (one per future Dose, ≤ a few per day for a typical Patient); the `WorkManager` periodic workers run at the 15-minute Android minimum per `_context/12 § Configuration And Secrets` row (a) with `Constraints.NONE` (reliability outweighs battery optimization for a medication-adherence product per the same row's rationale). No specific battery-drain target is committed; if a future quarter shows the app on Android's battery-drain top-10 list, this row revisits.
    - **On-device network**: **`Not applicable`** — no network usage per `_context/05 § Data protection` sub-rule (b).
